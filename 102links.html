ITS OK TO ASK FOR HELP
You are not alone...

In todays world, it is easy to feel overwhelmed and isolated. But remember, it's okay to ask for help. Whether you're struggling with personal issues, mental health, addiction, domestic violence or just need someone to talk to, there are resources available and always someone out there who knows and cares enough to help no matter how hopeless or helpless a situation may seem there is always a way through it but you can't always do it alone.

Thats what the organisations below are for...

Please browse and click any of these links that may apply to you or your situation, and please remember these important truths YOU ARE ENOUGH and YOU ARE WORTHY.

  • LIFELINE Life line is there to help and support if you are struggling with mental health - Click here to go to LifeLine
  • HEALTHPOINT Healthpoint is a mental health help line there to support you 24/7 Click here to go to HealthPoint
  • ANXIETYNZ Anxiety help line is another free service there to support anyone struggling with mental health Click here to go to AnxietyNZ
  • PARENTHELP ParentHelp is a compassionate non judgemental service there to give advice and support to parents Click here to go to ParentHelp
  • PARENT2PARENT Another page of links and hotlines to support parents Click here to go to Parent2Parent
  • ALCOHOL&DRUG HELP Alcohol & Drug Help is only a call away there to help people struggling with addiction Click here to go to Alcohol&DrugHelp
  • SALVATIONARMY BRIDGEPROGRAM Salvation Army Bridge program is a publicly funded residential rehibilatation center there to help people break the cycle of addiction Click here to apply for your spot at SalvationArmy BridgeProgram
  • AUCKLANDCITYMISSION Auckland City Mission provide comprehensive support for addicts including social & medical detox Click here to go to AucklandCityMission
